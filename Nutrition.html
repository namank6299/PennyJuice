Getting an internship or receiving a message that reads, "Congratulations, you have been selected for this internship or job," is something every aspiring intern or job candidate hopes to find in their inbox, isn't it? But have you ever considered what makes us capable of securing the right internships?

I'm not considering any of the hard skills mentioned in the role's description or responsibilities of an intern. So, what am I talking about? If you guessed, you're absolutely right.

I'm talking about the few questions that are typically asked of us every time we aim to secure an internship. In this piece of content, I have discussed some key aspects to keep in mind while applying for internships that can significantly enhance your chances of getting internship.


Things to consider while applying for an internship:

Let’s put all of these into the Steps and start it with.

Step-1:

Selection or Filtration of those internships that align with the skills and experience you possess. Upon doing so, whenever you step into an internship, you will feel inspired for your internship journey.

Now, it’s time for Step-2:

After selecting the right internship based on your skill set, review the role and responsibilities you will be assigned during the internship.

However, don’t limit yourself to this step. Visit the employer’s website or social media pages or whatever they have exposed on the platform to collect relevant information, such as their specific field and target audience.

In terms of content writing, this information might include the length of content, frequency of published content, and the importance of images and videos in the content.

This step may seem like a typical suggestion, it will be immensely helpful during your internship application process. Specifically, this information will assist you in crafting concise and efficient responses to the questions you will encounter alongside your internship application.”

Let’s move on to the third one or the Step-3.

This step seems to be crucial in terms of the internship, I am saying it’s crucial because it involves answering a few important questions that create your first impression on your employer.

As we face questions like –‘Why should you want to be hired for this role?’, we often try to get rid of the question by answering it as soon as possible, irrespective of whether the answer is relevant to our application or not. No, please don’t do that.

So the question arises: What should we do? Well, that’s a very simple answer, which you can find by just looking at the text box in which you are going to answer these questions. Let me explain what I mean to say. When you look carefully at the text box, you will find some simple but interesting points mentioned there in the form of placeholders.

For all those who don’t know what a placeholder is: a placeholder is a form of text written in a text box to describe the thing which you should write inside the text box. You can see the placeholder within a text box in the image provided below.


Coming back to our main theme, the points mentioned in the text box include:

  1. What skills or past experience do you have?

  2. What excites you about the internship for which you are applying?

Well, as per my own experience, I have also included one more point in the list and arranged them in the following way:

  1. What excites you about the internship for which you are applying?

  2. What relevant skills or past experience do you have?

  3. Introduce yourself as a person. I mean to say, the status of your soft skills, which may include ethics, communication, interpersonal skills, etc.

You can also include many more points that you think would work for you and your application.

Another important thing I want to mention here is that as you write answers with consideration of all these points, make sure that they align with the internship you are applying. These internships might be content writing or something else.”

After addressing this question, let's move on to the next one. Whenever an employer requests you to provide work samples, do not leave this section empty, as it will convey to the employer that you are not genuinely interested in the internship. Furthermore, it could have a detrimental impact on your application.

Instead of leaving the section blank, populate it with links that showcase your work samples. This approach will provide the employer with a clear demonstration of your technical capabilities and your ability to fulfil their expectations.

After completing all these steps, an applicant think their work is done, but no, it's not over yet. There is one more thing that requires your attention, and that is your resume. Yes, an employer also checks your resume after reviewing your application to make a final decision on whether to select or reject you.

So, what can you do to increase your chances of being selected? Keep updating your resume with the latest skills, certifications, experiences, projects, etc. This continuous improvement of your resume demonstrates to your employer that you are not only capable of meeting their expectations but also that you are committed to learning, growing, and updating yourself.

Additionally, this will also give your employer trust with your application or ultimately on you that you are truly going to be a right candidate for this role.

Conclusion:

In summary, securing an internship is not a difficult task; all we need to do is take care of every single step during the application process with calmness.
